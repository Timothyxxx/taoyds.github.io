I am a final-year PhD student in Computer Science at Yale University advised by Dragomir Radev. I am a member of LILY lab. I work in the area of Natural Language Processing (NLP). More specifically, my current research interest is to design and build conversational natural language interfaces (NLIs) that can help humans explore and reason over data in any application (e.g., relational databases and mobile apps) in a robust and trusted manner. It involves studies in semantic parsing, dialogue systems, human computer interaction, and robotics.

Check out our workshop on Interactive and Executable Semantic Parsing (IntEx-SemPar 2020) located with EMNLP 2020!

Please check out our Semantic Parsing/Text-to-SQL Challenge Series for building robust conversational NLIs:

  1. Spider: Yale Complex and Cross-Domain Semantic Parsing and Text-to-SQL Challenge (EMNLP 2018)
  2. SParC: Yale & Salesforce Semantic Parsing and Text-to-SQL in Context Challenge (ACL 2019)
  3. CoSQL: Yale & Salesforce Conversational Semantic Parsing and Text-to-SQL Challenge (EMNLP 2019)

Before coming to Yale, I completed my master's at Columbia University, where I worked on the BeST project for TAC KBP 2016 with Owen Rambow and Kathleen McKeown. Even before that, I was an undergraduate at the University of Utah.
